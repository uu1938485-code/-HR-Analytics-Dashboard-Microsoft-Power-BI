# HR-Analytics-Dashboard-Microsoft-Power-BI
![image]( https://github.com/uu1938485-code/-HR-Analytics-Dashboard-Microsoft-Power-BI/blob/5c1747bbc960dd94ef70bc528e6dbf9e72613214/Sales%20Dashboard%202026_page-0001.jpg)

 just completed my first ever Power BI Dashboard — an interactive HR Analytics report analyzing employee attrition patterns, salary distribution, age groups, and departmental insights across an organization of 1,480 employees!
 
 Dashboard Highlights:
 
  KPI Cards at a Glance
Total Employees: 1,480 | Average Age: 37 | Average Salary: 6.5K | Average Years at Company: 7.0 — giving instant visibility into workforce health
  Attrition by Education Field
Donut chart revealing that Life Sciences (41%) employees have the highest attrition, followed by Medical (31.76%), Marketing (10.88%), Technical Degree (8.92%) and Other (5.61%) — showing which talent pools are most at risk
  Attrition by Age Group
Column chart showing the 26–35 age group has the highest attrition at 611 employees, followed by 36–45 (471), 46–55 (228), 18–25 (123) and 55+ (47) — mid-career employees are leaving the most!
  Age Group by Job Role Matrix
Interactive table mapping all 9 job roles across 4 age groups — from Healthcare Representatives to Sales Executives — giving a complete workforce composition view
  Attrition by Salary
Bar chart revealing salary is a major attrition driver — employees earning up to 5K have the highest attrition at 753, followed by 5K–10K (444), 15K+ (150) and 10K–15K (133) — low salary = high turnover!
  Attrition by Job Role
Top roles losing employees:
  Sales Executive — 329
  Research Scientist — 293
  Laboratory Technician — 261
Manufacturing Director — 147
  Attrition by Year
Trend line showing attrition peaked at 198 then declined to 120, with fluctuations — valuable for spotting when HR interventions are needed
  Attrition by Gender
Male employees show higher attrition at 803 compared to Female at 518 — highlighting a gender retention gap
  Department Slicer
Interactive filter for Human Resources, Research & Development and Sales — allowing drill-down analysis per department instantly

  Key HR Insights Discovered:
  
  Low salary is the #1 attrition driver — 753 employees earning under 5K left
  26–35 age group is the most vulnerable — 611 attritions recorded
  Life Sciences graduates have the highest education-based attrition at 41%
  Sales Executives are the hardest hit role with 329 attritions
  Male employees leave more than female employees (803 vs 518)
  Average tenure before leaving is just 7 years — retention strategies needed
  Attrition peaked then dropped — suggesting HR policies had some positive impact
